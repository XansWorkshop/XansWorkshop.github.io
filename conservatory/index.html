Your journey began the moment you stepped foot within this universe...


The Conservatory is a narrative-backed, voxel-based universal sandbox game. This is my "final boss", metaphorically speaking; it puts the last 15 years of indie development experience to the ultimate test.

The Conservatory is not only an attempt to grow upon genres I like, but it's also a game for me personally. I want a way for the player to feel involved in the universe. I don't mean in the scope of "your actions have meaning" - that has been done for decades, that's just normal. Instead, I want the player to experience a level of wonder like you would get from going to a big city in person, exploring a new community center, travelling abroad... There's a certain sense of wonder, the kind you only get when you are experiencing a new world. That's the feeling I want to try to capture.

You see, I want a world that acknowledges who the player is. But vitally, I don't want it to be all serious and bureaucratic. We need to have fun. If I'm going to have a space station, I want niche and borderline comical accommodations for random races. I want multilingual boards in alien languages and codeblocks for synthetics. I want the player to go in and be able to react to the locale, it shouldn't ever feel "normal" to them
